Research Philosophy

Beginning with established models, our methodology emphasizes identifying which theoretical assumptions hold in practice, which can be refined, and which must be discarded.

A core pillar of our work is the use of data-driven modeling methods of complex systems to uncover hidden dynamics that cannot be captured by closed-form theory alone.

Work

We currently focus on three areas:

(1) Matrix representation of market state

Encoding order book and order-flow in a structured, efficient real-time use format.

(2) Short-term dynamics with DMD

Applying Dynamic Mode Decomposition to identify instantaneous microstructure regimes.

(3) Memory of past dynamics with Hopfield Networks

Storing and retrieving historical regimes to quickly recognize recurring microstructure patterns.

Tools & Open Source
