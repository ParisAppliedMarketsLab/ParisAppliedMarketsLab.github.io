About PAML

Paris Applied Markets Lab (PAML) is an independent research lab focused on market microstructure and applied algorithmic trading, in the same spirit that applied physics relates theory to real-world systems.

Starting from established models of order-driven markets, price formation, and execution, we study how they behave under real trading constraints: discrete limit order books, latency, queue priority, transaction costs, adverse selection, and adversarial order flow.

Our work emphasizes identifying which microstructure assumptions fail in practice, which can be repaired, and which must be discarded. PAML produces experimental research, execution studies, and open-source trading systems grounded in live and historical order-book data.

Tools & Open Source

We build and maintain open-source tools to test strategies, simulate markets, and evaluate execution under realistic conditions. All our research and infrastructure are available publicly to encourage reproducibility, collaboration, and feedback.

Philosophy

Markets are complex engineered systems. Models are useful only insofar as they survive contact with reality. PAML treats trading models as experiments: every result is tested under real constraints, documented, and reproduced. Our goal is to understand what works, why it works, and when it stops working.
