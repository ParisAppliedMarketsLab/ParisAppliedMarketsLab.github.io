Paris Applied Markets Lab

An independent research laboratory studying market microstructure and algorithmic trading. We bridge theoretical models with real-world systems through rigorous experimental research.

Research Philosophy

PAML conducts rigorous experimental research in market microstructure and algorithmic trading. Beginning with established models, we examine their behavior under real trading constraints including latency, queue priority, transaction costs and adverse selection.

Our methodology emphasizes identifying which theoretical assumptions hold in practice, which can be refined, and which must be discarded. All research outputs are documented with full transparency and grounded in live and historical order-book data.

Tools & Open Source

We develop open-source tools for market simulation, strategy testing, and execution evaluation under realistic conditions. All repositories are publicly available to encourage reproducibility and collaboration within the research community.

We welcome contributions, suggestions, and inquiries from researchers, practitioners, and anyone with interest in market microstructure or applied algorithmic trading.
